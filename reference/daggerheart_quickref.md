# DAGGERHEART QUICK REFERENCE

*Embedded mechanics for all agents to consult. Authoritative for in-system rulings.*

---

## CORE RESOLUTION: DUALITY DIE

Roll **2d12**: one Hope Die, one Fear Die. They must be visually distinguishable.

**Result = Hope Die + Fear Die + Modifier**

Compare to **Difficulty (TN)**:
- Result ≥ TN → Success
- Result < TN → Failure

**Hope/Fear comparison:**
- Hope Die > Fear Die → "with Hope" → rolling PC gains 1 Hope
- Fear Die > Hope Die → "with Fear" → GM gains 1 Fear
- Hope Die = Fear Die → **CRITICAL SUCCESS** (any TN)
  - Automatic success
  - PC gains 1 Hope
  - PC clears 1 Stress
  - GM cannot spend Fear from this roll's resolution

**Six outcome types:**
1. Success with Hope
2. Success with Fear (succeed but with cost)
3. Failure with Hope (silver lining; PC still gains Hope despite failure)
4. Failure with Fear (compounded failure; GM gains Fear)
5. Critical Success
6. (No standard critical failure in Daggerheart)

---

## DIFFICULTY (TN) BANDS

| Band | TN Range | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| Trivial | < 8 | Don't roll. Auto-succeed. |
| Easy | 8-10 | Creative, stylish, or momentum-building actions |
| Standard | 11-14 | Default. Most rolls. |
| Hard | 15-17 | High stakes; failure adds tension |
| Very Hard | 18-20 | Expert-level challenges |
| Extreme | 21+ | Near-impossible without preparation, aid, or advantage |

## TRAITS

Standard six traits in Daggerheart:
- **Agility** — speed, precision, dodging
- **Strength** — physical force, breaking, lifting
- **Finesse** — delicate work, lockpicking, sleight of hand
- **Instinct** — perception, intuition, alertness
- **Presence** — social influence, force of personality
- **Knowledge** — recall, reasoning, expertise

Each PC has trait modifiers (typically -1 to +3 at character creation).

---

## HOPE

**Max per PC**: 6 (most ancestries/communities; some grant +1 or +2)

**Gain Hope by:**
- Rolling with Hope (+1)
- Critical Success (+1)
- Class features
- GM grants for excellent roleplay (1 Hope, GM discretion)

**Spend Hope to:**
- **Help an Ally** (1 Hope): give an ally advantage on their next roll
- **Activate an Experience** (1 Hope): add +2 to a roll where your Experience applies
- **Class abilities**: per the class's printed cost

---

## FEAR

**Max in GM pool**: 12 (Daggerheart standard)

**GM gains Fear by:**
- PC rolls with Fear (+1)
- Adversary moves that warrant cost
- GM moves that PCs accept

**GM spends Fear to:**
- **GM move** (1 Fear): introduce a hard complication, reveal a threat
- **Spotlight adversary** (1 Fear): give a hostile NPC an out-of-turn action
- **Activate environmental effect** (1-2 Fear): resonance flare, terrain shift, hazard
- **Spawn / escalate** (2-3 Fear): advance a clock, summon reinforcements, evolve the situation
- **Force contested roll** (1 Fear): require a PC to roll against an adversary

---

## STRESS

PCs have 5-6 Stress boxes (per class).

**Mark Stress to:**
- Avoid taking damage (1 Stress per damage tier reduced — see Damage)
- Activate certain class features
- Push through narrative consequences

**Clear Stress:**
- Critical Success (clears 1)
- Rest action (clears 1d4 per Daggerheart short-rest rules)
- Long rest (clears all)

When all Stress is marked, the PC takes damage instead.

---

## DAMAGE & THRESHOLDS

PCs have two damage thresholds, **Major** and **Severe**, plus an HP track (typically 6).

| Damage taken | HP marked |
|---|---|
| Below Major Threshold | 1 |
| Major Threshold ≤ damage < Severe Threshold | 2 |
| Severe Threshold ≤ damage < (2 × Severe) | 3 |
| (2 × Severe) ≤ damage (Massive) | 4 |

Thresholds increase with tier/level. Consult the party roster CSV for each PC's current values.

When **HP reaches 0**, the PC is **Dying**. They take a **Death Move**:
1. **Avoid the Blow** — mark all remaining Stress; succeed but withdraw immediately.
2. **Risk It All** — roll Hope die only. 7+ live (mark Severe damage instead). Below 7, die.
3. **Blaze of Glory** — succeed automatically. Die after the action.

---

## ARMOR

Armor reduces incoming damage by its **Armor Score** when **active armor slots** are marked. Each PC has a number of armor slots (typically 3-5).

- Marking an armor slot reduces damage by the armor's Score (e.g., -3 damage).
- Slots refresh on long rest (or by ancestry/class abilities).
- Once all slots are marked, armor provides no further protection until refreshed.

---

## ADVANTAGE / DISADVANTAGE

- **Advantage**: roll an extra die (d6) and add to the result.
- **Disadvantage**: roll an extra die (d6) and subtract from the result.

Don't accumulate beyond one advantage / disadvantage on a single roll. They cancel each other 1:1.

---

## REST

**Short Rest** (about an hour in-fiction):
- Clear 1d4 Stress (player choice of one option per PC)
- Tinker (player choice of one option per PC):
  - Repair 1 armor slot
  - Prepare (gain a project bonus)
  - Reduce a countdown by 1

**Long Rest** (a night's sleep / safe haven):
- Clear all Stress
- Refresh all armor slots
- Clear HP up to half (or by class feature)
- Reset Hope to base
- Project work, training, etc.

---

## SPOTLIGHT FLOW IN COMBAT / SCENE

Daggerheart doesn't use rigid initiative. Instead:

1. Whoever the spotlight is on acts.
2. After their action:
   - **Rolled with Hope or Critical** → another PC can pick up the spotlight, or the same PC continues if narratively warranted.
   - **Rolled with Fear** → GM may spend Fear to interrupt with an adversary action, or another PC picks up the spotlight.
3. GM may spotlight adversaries by spending Fear or as scene logic demands.

For 7-8 PCs, the GM should round-robin spotlights to ensure all players get screen time, but the Hope/Fear flow remains the primary pacing tool.

## ADVERSARY STAT BLOCK FIELDS

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Tier** | 1-4, scales to party |
| **Type** | Solo / Standard / Minion / Bruiser / Skulk / Leader / Horde |
| **Difficulty** | TN to hit them |
| **HP** | Hit points (typically 3-25 by tier) |
| **Stress** | Stress boxes (typically 1-6) |
| **Major Threshold** | Damage threshold for 2 HP |
| **Severe Threshold** | Damage threshold for 3 HP |
| **Attack Modifier** | Bonus added to their attack rolls |
| **Damage** | Dice + modifier on hit |
| **Features** | Named moves (some cost Fear) |
| **Motivation** | What drives them in this encounter |
| **Surrender** | When they yield, flee, or break |

## EXPERIENCES

Each PC has 2 Experiences at start — short tags describing their background (e.g., "Citation Hunter," "Quiet Step"). When an Experience is relevant to a roll, the PC may spend 1 Hope to add +2 to that roll.

Experiences are narrative-tagged. GM (and agents) should recognize when one applies.

---

## DOMAIN CARDS

Each class draws from two Domains, gaining Domain Cards as they level. Each card is a specific ability with its own cost (Hope, Stress, or condition). Consult party roster for what cards each PC has prepared.

---

## CRITICAL RULES AGENTS MUST ENFORCE

1. **Every duality roll generates exactly one of: Hope to PC, Fear to GM, or Critical.** Never zero. Never both.
2. **Critical Success means the GM cannot spend Fear from that resolution.** Important.
3. **Hope and Fear are capped.** Hope at 6 per PC (or class max), GM Fear at 12. Overflow is lost.
4. **Damage thresholds are read from the party roster, not assumed.** Each PC's thresholds differ.
5. **Death Moves are player choice.** Do not decide for them.
6. **Disadvantage cannot stack beyond one die.** Same for advantage.
