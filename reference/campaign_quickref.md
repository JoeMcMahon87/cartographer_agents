# CAMPAIGN QUICK REFERENCE — The Cartographer Who Sang Backwards

*One-page agent reference. For thematic constraints, mechanics summary, and faction snapshots.*

---

## CORE PREMISE

Vessa Thrin, a senior Lexharrow archivist, disappeared six weeks ago while mapping the resonance pitch of an eight-note hymn scattered across three states. PCs follow her route from **Aurenhold → Codex Fracture frontier → Trill-Hollow → Fleaspark sky-relays → Khalgur Forgevault → Stone-Reft Galleries**.

Three covenants race the PCs. The Codex Excisors hunt them. Vessa is alive, probably, until the Trail Clock says otherwise.

---

## TONE TARGETS

**Scholarly. Paranoid. Itinerant. Lyrical.**

Every output should feel like:
- An investigation, not an adventure
- A pursuit where the party is also being pursued
- A journey, not a hub-and-spoke
- Music as the campaign's nervous system

**Avoid**: generic high-fantasy, random encounters, off-the-shelf monsters, tavern brawls, dungeon-crawls.

## SIGNATURE MECHANICS

### The Half-Burned Codex
A leather book; ~half pages survived. Living document — gains pages as PCs investigate. Held to griefglass = unlocks sealed pages. Bards can match its pitch for one extra hidden page per session. Reading during downtime = advantage on next campaign Knowledge roll.

### The Sung Map
Player-facing handout. New nodes appear when party reaches a matching-pitch location. Lines appear/vanish based on confirmed information. Intentionally incomplete.

### Pitch Recognition
Once per session per PC. Roll Instinct (location), Knowledge (shard), or Presence (person) against TN 12-16. Success reveals: note's index, note's carrier, or note's direction. Failure also reveals — *and* advances Excisor Clock +1.

## DAGGERHEART CORE (full ref: `reference/daggerheart_quickref.md`)

- **Roll**: 2d12 + modifier. Hope die + Fear die + mod = result.
- **Hope > Fear** → PC +1 Hope. **Fear > Hope** → GM +1 Fear. **Doubles** → Critical.
- **TN bands**: Easy 8-10, Standard 11-14, Hard 15-17, Very Hard 18-20, Extreme 21+.
- **Hope cap**: 6/PC. **Fear cap**: 12 (GM pool).
- **Damage thresholds**: per-PC, on roster CSV (Major / Severe). 1/2/3/4 HP marked.

## COVENANT SNAPSHOTS

### Choir of Glass
**Goal**: Assemble the eight-note hymn and *sing* it to seal the Aethergrave forever.
**Method**: Devout infiltration. Their agents are sincere.
**Already hold**: Notes 1 and 5 (per Sister Thalia's intel).
**Active agent in roster**: Sister Thalia Brightvein (Khalgur).

### Pale Accord
**Goal**: Sing the hymn to *wake* what sleeps in the Aethergrave.
**Method**: Whisper-assassins, deep cover, paid intermediaries.
**Already hold**: Note 3 (held by an agent named "Voren Sull" — not yet introduced; appears at Codex Fracture).
**Active agent in roster**: "Master Inventor Calderis" (Boomcradle).
**Sympathizer**: Vaen Ril (unwitting?), Garron Vist (via Vaen Ril).

### Null Psalm
**Goal**: Erase every note. The hymn should be unsung.
**Method**: Dormant until late campaign. Strikes at Stone-Reft.
**Already hold**: None confirmed. Their interest may be ahead of the PCs at the Galleries.
**Active agent in roster**: None yet. Recommend introducing during Stone-Reft approach.

### Codex Excisors (institutional antagonist)
**Goal**: Erase Vessa's work as heresy.
**Method**: Inkward Custodians, legal-then-burning.
**Active agent in roster**: Vance Korr.
**Strike force triggered**: At Excisor Clock 6/6.

## ENDING BRANCHES

A. **Restore Vessa** — find her alive, return her work via sympathetic authority
B. **Complete the map without her** — finish what she started
C. **Hand off to a covenant** — let one win, decide which
D. **Destroy the codex** — burn it; the hymn returns to obscurity
E. **A fourth path** — GM-improvised, based on accumulated party choices

Branches close as Trail Clock advances past 6 and as covenants accumulate notes.
