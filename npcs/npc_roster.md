## STAGE 1 — AURENHOLD (Lexharrow capital)

### Vessa Thrin
- **Role**: The missing archivist. Campaign's central absent figure.
- **Ancestry / Community**: Human, Loreborne
- **Location**: Unknown until late campaign (last known: en route to Stone-Reft Galleries)
- **Voice**: lyrical, exhausted, certain, sings phrases mid-sentence, speaks like someone alone with her thoughts too long
- **Goal**: Complete her resonance-pitch map of the eight hymn-notes; understand what the hymn is *for* before any covenant claims it
- **Knows**: Everything the codex contains, plus what she has deliberately not written down (location of the second codex, names of two living note-carriers, the cellar-cache pattern)
- **Relationship to Vessa**: (self)
- **Faction tie**: None. She has actively refused all three covenants. Vaen Ril knows she has refused.
- **Hook**: She named the PCs in her recourse clause. Why? They will eventually have to ask her.
- **Disposition**: Friendly (presumed, until met). May shift sharply when actually encountered.
- **GM note**: Do not produce on-screen Vessa dialogue until the GM confirms the party has reached her. Her presence is in marginalia, half-burned letters, cat, and the doubts of those who knew her.

### Mirelle Aune-Vesk
- **Role**: Vessa's senior assistant. Inciting-incident NPC.
- **Ancestry / Community**: Human, Loreborne
- **Location**: Aurenhold, Quill District (initially); may move to Trill-Hollow within 2-3 weeks of campaign start
- **Voice**: precise, anxious, parenthetical, scholarly tic of self-correction, sentences with embedded corrections — "It was — well, not exactly that, but close enough — three nights ago. Or four."
- **Goal**: Survive. Get the codex out of Aurenhold. See Vessa safe (in that order).
- **Knows**: Vessa's three assistants (her, Joren, Salah) and the fate of the other two; that Vaen Ril inquired about Vessa twice in the last month; that a second codex exists somewhere outside the Archive (won't share until Friendly)
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Devoted junior colleague; not romantic, but the kind of professional love that runs deeper
- **Faction tie**: None. The Custodians have her noted but not yet flagged.
- **Hook**: She finds the party in Aurenhold and hands over the satchel. She is the campaign's first thread to the codex.
- **Disposition**: Neutral (cautious gratitude)

### Arch-Chronicler Vaen Ril
- **Role**: Reformer of the Lexharrow Archive. Vessa's nominal superior. Worldbook-canonical.
- **Ancestry / Community**: Human, Highborne (Orderborne by office)
- **Location**: The Codex Bastion, Aurenhold
- **Voice**: reformist, careful, every word politically calibrated, never quite says no, answers questions with other questions
- **Goal**: Modernize the Archive by digitizing memory through griefglass lattices. Vessa's work either *proves* his theories or *destroys* his political viability. He has not decided which.
- **Knows**: Vessa filed false travel papers; he countersigned them anyway. He has read parts of her early research. He does not know about the second codex.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Mentor-figure. May be ally, opportunist, or active threat — GM decides based on party play.
- **Faction tie**: Officially none. Quietly: he has corresponded with a Pale Accord scholar, ostensibly about griefglass digitization theory. He believes the correspondence is academic. It may not be.
- **Hook**: PCs will need archive access at some point. He grants or denies it. He will eventually request a meeting.
- **Disposition**: Wary (default — his interest in the party is real but provisional)

### Vance Korr
- **Role**: Inkward Custodian. Antagonist enforcer.
- **Ancestry / Community**: Human, Orderborne
- **Location**: Mobile — wherever the Excisors send him; first appears at Aurenhold's north gate
- **Voice**: bureaucratic, certain, addresses people by surname when possible, reads from his brass-bound ledger before looking up, does not blink unnecessarily
- **Goal**: Recover the codex. File the necessary paperwork to make the recovery legal after the fact.
- **Knows**: Vessa is on a strike-through register; the party has been flagged as "associates of interest"; the Custodians are aware the codex left the Archive
- **Relationship to Vessa**: None personal. He has read her name many times without meeting her.
- **Faction tie**: Codex Excisors (Inkward Custodian rank)
- **Hook**: First gate check; recurs at threshold-3 of Excisor Clock and as the strike-force leader at 6/6
- **Disposition**: Wary → Hostile (will shift Hostile if PCs reveal their purpose, lie clumsily, or attract a second Custodian's attention)

### Joren Vell (the Glossarist Rival)
- **Role**: Vessa's academic rival. Glossarist scholar.
- **Ancestry / Community**: Faerie, Loreborne
- **Location**: Aurenhold, Glossarist halls; available throughout Stage 1
- **Voice**: cutting, dryly amused, name-drops citations to wound, fond of the word "naturally"
- **Goal**: Be proven right about Vessa being a wasted talent. Secondary: enjoy being asked to comment on her absence.
- **Knows**: That Vessa was reading material outside her authorization for the last two years; that she once submitted a paper anonymously to a Tarkhos ember-journal and was rejected (he was the rejecting reviewer, though she doesn't know that)
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Public colleague, private rival. The kind of person who will say "we were close" at her memorial without flinching.
- **Faction tie**: None directly. Sympathetic to Excisor positions ("standards must be standards").
- **Hook**: PCs investigating Vessa's academic history are routed to him. He'll talk. The price is hearing his interpretation of her shortcomings.
- **Disposition**: Neutral (transactional — happy to chat for the price of his vanity being fed)

## STAGE 2 — CODEX FRACTURE FRONTIER (northern Lexharrow)

### Garron Vist
- **Role**: Waystation keeper at Garron's Quietstone.
- **Ancestry / Community**: Human, Loreborne (ex-archivist clerk)
- **Location**: His waystation, two days north of Aurenhold
- **Voice**: careful, soft, never wastes a syllable, the long pauses of a man who decided years ago to say less than he knows
- **Goal**: Be left alone. Keep his cellar cache intact. Avoid the Excisors noticing him.
- **Knows**: Vessa stayed at his waystation for two nights and wiped her chalk-marks before leaving. She rode east toward the Wakewall foothills. He has three crates of resonance-charged griefglass shards in his cellar — left in his keeping by *someone* (he won't name them).
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Distant. He admired her work from the periphery. He once filed her travel paper. He thinks she should have stayed in the lower stacks.
- **Faction tie**: Cooperates intermittently with the Pale Accord — though he tells himself he is only doing a favor for an old acquaintance (Vaen Ril)
- **Hook**: First named waystop on the trail. Knows where Vessa went next.
- **Disposition**: Wary

### Sister Anike Voss
- **Role**: Echo Warden survivor, exiled to the frontier
- **Ancestry / Community**: Dwarf, Underborne
- **Location**: A roving camp along the Codex Fracture; finds the party or is found
- **Voice**: clipped, military, drops articles ("Heard the same hum. Three weeks back. South ridge."), uses "the lattice" as shorthand for all of Vessa's research without realizing she's using a specialized term
- **Goal**: Find out why Vessa stopped writing to her. Honor a debt she owed Vessa's father.
- **Knows**: Vessa visited her camp briefly. Vessa was tracking *specific* griefglass pitches, not all of them. The Echo Warden chapter in Khalgur had a file on Vessa that was deleted last month.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Friend of Vessa's late father; honorary aunt-figure to Vessa
- **Faction tie**: Loyal to Echo Wardens, but currently outside their command structure (exile is voluntary, after a disagreement she won't discuss)
- **Hook**: PCs in the Fracture for more than a day will be intercepted by her scouts. She tests them before sharing.
- **Disposition**: Wary → Friendly (shifts quickly if PCs pass her tests — competence and discretion both)

### Pell Ash-and-Soot
- **Role**: Hollowbound traveler with hymn-trace
- **Ancestry / Community**: Hollowbound, Wanderborne
- **Location**: Anywhere along the trail; appears repeatedly
- **Voice**: slow, considered, speaks of the body in third person ("the bones remember"), pauses to listen mid-sentence as if checking with someone else
- **Goal**: Find out what is humming inside them. Maybe stop it. Maybe not.
- **Knows**: Carries a hymn-note fragment in their resonance scars — *they are themselves a piece of evidence*. They do not know which note. They feel it as a pressure behind the sternum that changes when they walk certain directions.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: None directly. Vessa noted "the walking shard" in her marginalia but never identified them by name.
- **Faction tie**: None — but the Pale Accord has begun watching Pell. The Choir would kill to study them.
- **Hook**: A walking Proof Clue. Recurs. PCs who help them gain a powerful ally and a covenant target.
- **Disposition**: Neutral (curious about anyone who notices what they carry)

## STAGE 3 — FLEASPARK UNION (Boomcradle and sky-relays)

### Captain Quenn Mirebright
- **Role**: Boomwright sky-skiff pilot
- **Ancestry / Community**: Goblin, Wanderborne
- **Location**: Boomcradle sky-docks; flies the Aurenhold-Khalgur relay route
- **Voice**: rapid, contractive ("y'gonna wanna," "lemme tell ya"), interrupts self with technical asides, swears in patent-numbers
- **Goal**: Make a living. Don't lose another ship. Buy out their indenture to the Patent Court.
- **Knows**: Flew Vessa to a relay station two months ago. Vessa was sharing the skiff with one other passenger — a quiet man with a calligrapher's hands who paid in Aurex pre-stamped coin. Quenn does not know his name; they remember his ring (a sigil that matches no faction they know).
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Professional. Vessa tipped well. Quenn liked her.
- **Faction tie**: None. The unnamed passenger may have been Pale Accord — let the party investigate.
- **Hook**: Cheapest air-passage onward. They will fly the party for the right price.
- **Disposition**: Friendly (transactional but warm — willing to share if the party doesn't waste their time)

### Patent-Clerk Mox Skellig
- **Role**: Goblin patent-clerk in Boomcradle's Patent Court
- **Ancestry / Community**: Goblin, Highborne (Court-class)
- **Location**: Boomcradle Patent Court, daytime only
- **Voice**: officious, fond of dramatic pauses, uses "we" to mean "the Court," takes pleasure in correcting other people's terminology
- **Goal**: Be promoted. Avoid the irregularity Vessa created in their filing system.
- **Knows**: Vessa filed an irregular patent inquiry six weeks ago — for the chemical signature of a specific griefglass pitch. The patent was rejected. The form is still in the rejection drawer. Mox has not destroyed it because they are afraid of who might come asking.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: One transaction. Memorable because of the irregularity.
- **Faction tie**: None. Will sell the form to the highest bidder if pressed. The Excisors have not asked yet.
- **Hook**: Anyone tracking Vessa's paper trail through Fleaspark hits Mox. The form is here.
- **Disposition**: Neutral (transactional, slightly nervous)

### "Master Inventor Calderis" (Pale Accord agent)
- **Role**: Pale Accord whisper-assassin disguised as a kite-wright
- **Ancestry / Community**: Human, Highborne (claimed) — actual background unknown
- **Location**: Boomcradle, runs a small kite-wright workshop near the sky-docks
- **Voice**: charming, articulate, slightly old-fashioned vocabulary ("delighted," "indeed," "if I may"), perfect manners that never crack
- **Goal**: Acquire whatever Vessa was carrying. Eliminate witnesses if necessary. Keep his cover intact for future Fleaspark operations.
- **Knows**: That Vessa carried at least one identified note (note 3 — the pitch sealed in a Boomcradle reactor decades ago). That Mirelle is alive in Aurenhold. That the PCs are following the trail.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: He arranged the air-passage that took her onward — by paying the kite-wright who built Quenn's skiff to recommend that pilot. He met her once, briefly, in Boomcradle. She did not realize who he was.
- **Faction tie**: Pale Accord (whisper-assassin rank, deep cover)
- **Hook**: Helpful kite-wright who happens to know everyone. PCs investigating Boomcradle will be steered toward him. He invites them to tea.
- **Disposition**: Friendly (cover) → Hostile (when cover breaks)
- **GM note**: If exposed, he flees rather than fighting unless cornered. He values future operations over this mission.

## STAGE 4 — GRAVENREACH (Khalgur Forgevault and Stone-Reft approach)

### Iron-Warden Brann Tessik
- **Role**: Echo Warden stationed at Khalgur Forgevault
- **Ancestry / Community**: Dwarf, Orderborne
- **Location**: The Great Anvil Hall, Khalgur; the Wakewall foothill watchposts
- **Voice**: formal, careful with oaths, refers to laws by their striking-date ("the Clarification of 318 BP holds that…"), pauses before agreeing to anything
- **Goal**: Contain the resonance instability in the Wakewall. Prevent another Stone-Reft incident.
- **Knows**: Khalgur's archives had a file on Vessa that was deleted last month (per Anike Voss). Brann saw the deletion order — countersigned by an Echo Warden he respects. He has not asked why.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: He met her once. She asked good questions about Stone-Seal protocols. He liked her.
- **Faction tie**: Echo Wardens (loyal). Whether the Echo Wardens are *themselves* compromised is one of the campaign's late questions.
- **Hook**: Official Khalgur contact. May help or impede depending on disposition. Knows about the Stone-Reft Galleries entrance.
- **Disposition**: Neutral (wary of outsiders, but professional)

### Old Mother Velka Stonevault
- **Role**: Stonevault deep-singer; has heard hymn-tones in stone before
- **Ancestry / Community**: Dwarf, Underborne (Stonevault lineage)
- **Location**: A harmonic well chamber in lower Khalgur; reached by ritual invitation
- **Voice**: low, slow, sings half her sentences without realizing, refers to PCs as "you who came up the wrong way," uses "the long ago" to mean any time before her grandmother
- **Goal**: Pass the lineage's resonance-knowledge to someone who will not break it. She has no Stonevault heirs left who will listen.
- **Knows**: She has heard *two* of the eight hymn-notes during her life — once as a child (in stone), once as a young singer (in a Dwarf who later went mad). She will share what they sounded like, if she trusts the listener.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Vessa came to her three years ago and was refused — Velka did not trust her enough yet. Velka has regretted it since.
- **Faction tie**: None. Old enough to remember when there was only one Concord.
- **Hook**: PCs who learn she exists must earn an audience. She lives in the harmonic well and does not come up.
- **Disposition**: Wary (will not engage unless Vessa or Brann vouches for the party)

### Sister Thalia Brightvein (Choir of Glass infiltrator)
- **Role**: Choir of Glass agent posing as a Solivar acolyte
- **Ancestry / Community**: Elf, Orderborne (cover) — actual community concealed
- **Location**: Khalgur diplomatic hostels; moves to wherever the party is
- **Voice**: gentle, devout-sounding, quotes Concord-aligned scripture (accurately — she has studied), uses "the seven" instead of "the Choir" when slipping
- **Goal**: Identify which hymn-notes the party has confirmed and report back. Recruit a sympathetic PC if possible. Eliminate the codex if cornered.
- **Knows**: Thalia is the Choir's most placed agent in Gravenreach. She knows the Choir already holds two notes (1 and 5). She knows the Pale Accord is also active. She does not know where Vessa is — and is hoping the party will lead her there.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Never met her. Has read everything the Choir has on her.
- **Faction tie**: Choir of Glass (sworn voice)
- **Hook**: She approaches the party as a friendly fellow traveler at a Khalgur hostel. She is helpful, kind, deeply pious-seeming. She is exactly what they want when they are tired.
- **Disposition**: Friendly (cover) → Hostile (when exposed) → ??? (she may be turnable; the Choir's theology is not insincere)

### Drask Vor (Drakona auxiliary)
- **Role**: Drakona auxiliary serving Echo Wardens; conflicted loyalty
- **Ancestry / Community**: Drakona, Orderborne (Drakona clan affiliation as primary)
- **Location**: Echo Warden patrols along the Wakewall; can be requested as an escort
- **Voice**: clipped, short sentences, addresses people by title, lapses into Drakona phrases when emotional (which he tries to hide), pronounces "Vessa" carefully because the soft sounds are difficult for his speech
- **Goal**: Honor his clan's debt to the Echo Wardens *and* honor his clan's deeper debt to the resonance research Vessa was doing (one of his cousins is Hollowbound — like Pell). He cannot honor both.
- **Knows**: The Echo Wardens have orders to detain Vessa if they find her. He has not been told why. He suspects.
- **Relationship to Vessa**: Met her once. She asked about his cousin. She remembered the name.
- **Faction tie**: Drakona clan (deepest); Echo Wardens (formal). Will choose clan if forced. PCs who frame their mission as preserving Hollowbound dignity may win him over.
- **Hook**: Assigned to escort the party into the Stone-Reft approach. He is their nominal handler. He is also their best chance of getting in.
- **Disposition**: Wary → Friendly (shifts based on how the party treats Pell, if Pell is in the party, or any other Hollowbound NPC)
